Privacy Policy – 3LINX Fulfillment Integration

Effective Date: January 15, 2025  |  Last Updated: January 15, 2025

1. Introduction

3LINX Fulfillment Integration ("App", "we", "our") provides automated fulfillment services through Shopify. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, store, share, and protect data when merchants install or use the App.

This document covers all data processing activities: store information, product catalog, inventory, orders, protected customer data, technical logs, and support communications.

By installing the App, you confirm that you have authority to accept this Privacy Policy on behalf of your business and to inform your customers about the processing activities described.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Store Information

  • Store name, domain, Shopify ID, owner contact details
  • Time zone, currency, plan and subscription details
  • API tokens and configuration data

2.2 Product and Inventory Data

  • Product SKUs, titles, descriptions, variants, weights, HSCodes
  • Inventory levels, movements, reservations, transfers, audit logs

2.3 Orders and Transactions

  • Order IDs, line items, totals, taxes, status, currency
  • Shipping method, gift messages, cancellations, returns, fraud flags

2.4 Protected Customer Data (Level 2)

  • First and last names
  • Email addresses and phone numbers
  • Billing and shipping addresses, delivery preferences
  • Order history linked to fulfillment

2.5 Shipping and Fulfillment Data

  • Carrier choices, labels, tracking numbers, customs forms, proof of delivery, RMAs

2.6 Technical and Support Data

  • API/webhook logs, uptime metrics, error messages
  • Support tickets, emails, chat logs, call recordings (with consent)

3. How We Use Data

  • Process and route orders to 3LINX distribution centers
  • Synchronize inventory in real time
  • Generate labels, coordinate carriers, handle returns
  • Provide tracking and customer notifications via merchants
  • Improve performance and optimize operations
  • Comply with tax, customs, and regulatory obligations
  • Protect security, prevent fraud, and monitor service health

We do not use customer data for advertising or cross-selling.

4. Legal Basis

  • Contractual necessity: perform fulfillment services requested by merchants
  • Legitimate interests: fraud prevention, optimization, service improvement
  • Legal compliance: customs, tax, record-keeping, regulatory reporting
  • Consent: where required by law

5. Data Sharing

5.1 Fulfillment Centers

3LINX warehouses (US and Canada) receive data strictly for order preparation, shipping, and returns.

5.2 Carriers

UPS, FedEx, USPS, Canada Post, and regional carriers receive only the data required to complete deliveries.

5.3 Technology Providers

Cloud hosting, monitoring, database management, and analytics vendors under strict DPAs.

5.4 Professional Services

Legal counsel, auditors, accountants, and security consultants, when required.

5.5 Restrictions

  • No selling of data
  • No sharing with ad networks or social platforms
  • No marketing use

6. Data Security

6.1 Encryption

  • TLS 1.2+ in transit
  • AES-256 at rest
  • Encrypted backups, rotated and geo-distributed

6.2 Access Control

  • Role-based access (least privilege)
  • Multi-factor authentication
  • Privileged Access Management for admins
  • Logs of all access to protected customer data

6.3 Infrastructure

  • Firewalls, IDS/IPS, DDoS protection
  • Segregated environments (dev/test/prod)
  • Regular patching and vulnerability scans

6.4 Monitoring & Incident Response

  • SIEM monitoring and anomaly detection
  • Incident classification, containment, forensic analysis
  • Breach notification within required timelines (72h GDPR)

7. Retention and Deletion

7.1 Retention Periods

  • Orders and invoices: 7 years (tax/legal)
  • Shipping/tracking: 5 years
  • Technical logs: 1–3 years
  • Support tickets: 5 years
  • Security incidents: 7 years

7.2 Deletion Procedures

  • Cryptographic erasure for encrypted data
  • Multi-pass overwriting or physical destruction of hardware
  • Removal from backups after retention cycle
  • Certificate of deletion available upon request

7.3 Uninstallation

  • 30-day grace period after uninstall
  • Notification to merchant before permanent deletion
  • Secure deletion of all merchant and customer data

8. International Transfers

Data may be transferred to the US, Canada, EU, and UK. Safeguards include:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • UK IDTA
  • Supplementary technical and organizational measures

9. Rights of Data Subjects

Depending on jurisdiction (GDPR, CCPA, etc.), individuals may request:

  • Access, rectification, deletion
  • Restriction or objection to processing
  • Portability in machine-readable format
  • Human review of automated decisions

Requests are handled within statutory timelines (30 days GDPR, 45 days CCPA). Merchants remain primary controllers, and we support them in fulfilling requests.

10. Children’s Privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly process data of minors.

11. Changes

We may update this policy periodically. Merchants will be notified in advance of material changes.

Annex A – Shopify Protected Customer Data Compliance

Levels of Data Use

  • Level 1: customer data excluding name, email, phone, address
  • Level 2: customer data including name, email, phone, address

Our Practices

  • We request only the minimum required fields: first/last name, email, phone, shipping/billing address.
  • We handle redacted fields gracefully if Shopify denies access.
  • We respect consent and opt-out decisions from merchants and customers.
  • We provide transparency and data minimization in line with Shopify’s requirements.

Level 1 Requirements (Implemented)

  • Process only data needed for functionality
  • Inform merchants of purpose and scope
  • Limit processing to stated purposes
  • Apply retention periods
  • Encrypt data in transit and at rest
  • Respect consent and opt-out decisions

Level 2 Requirements (Implemented)

  • Encrypt backups
  • Keep test and production data separate
  • Maintain a DLP strategy
  • Restrict staff access and enforce MFA/password policies
  • Maintain access logs for protected data
  • Implement security incident response policies

Annex B – API Scopes and Webhooks

API Scopes Requested

  • read_customers, read_orders, read_fulfillments, write_fulfillments
  • read_inventory, write_inventory
  • read_locations, read_shipping

Webhooks Subscribed

  • orders/create, orders/updated, orders/fulfilled, orders/cancelled
  • fulfillments/create, fulfillments/update
  • inventory_levels/update

Annex D – Uninstallation & Deletion Workflow

  1. Merchant uninstalls app via Shopify.
  2. App flags account for deletion.
  3. Merchant notified of upcoming deletion.
  4. Optional data export offered.
  5. 30-day grace period for accidental uninstall.
  6. Secure deletion of production data.
  7. Removal from backups per cycle.
  8. Confirmation of deletion provided upon request.

Annex E – Breach Notification Procedures

  • Detection: continuous monitoring, SIEM alerts, vulnerability management.
  • Classification: severity scale (low, medium, high, critical).
  • Response: containment, eradication, forensic analysis.
  • Notification: within 72h to supervisory authority (GDPR), within statutory deadlines for other jurisdictions. Merchants notified immediately if affected.
  • Remediation: root cause analysis, lessons learned, improvements.

Annex F – International Transfers Safeguards

  • SCCs incorporated into DPAs with all vendors.
  • UK IDTA applied for UK transfers.
  • Transfer Impact Assessments performed regularly.
  • Technical safeguards: encryption, MFA, access logging.
  • Organizational safeguards: staff training, vendor audits, compliance monitoring.
